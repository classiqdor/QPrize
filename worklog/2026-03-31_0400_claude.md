### Cadmium source exploration — KEY DISCOVERY

Explored `/repos/Cadmium/python_sdk` for hidden levers. Found:

**`Constraints.optimization_parameter` was never activated!**

Default is `OptimizationParameter.NO_OPTIMIZATION`. We've been using `Constraints(max_width=500)` which leaves optimization disabled. Two new options:
1. `OptimizationParameter.DEPTH` — minimize circuit depth
2. `TranspilerBasisGates.CX` — minimize CX gate count directly (most targeted!)

Also found: `transpilation_option=TranspilationOption.INTENSIVE` is independent from `optimization_level` and applies more aggressive transpilation passes.

**Modular arithmetic finding**: No cheaper quantum×quantum multiplication exists. `modular_multiply_constant` (QFT-space, cheaper) only works when one operand is a classical constant. All ECDLP multiplications involve two quantum operands.

## Key lessons

1. **`optimization_parameter=NO_OPTIMIZATION` is the default** — we've been synthesizing without explicit CX/depth optimization for all 4 weeks. New axes discovered.

2. **optimization_level ≤ 2 doesn't help** — level 1 and 2 produce identical CX (both at local optimum).

3. **6-bit ECDLP can't synthesize at level 1** within 3600s. Either need level 0 + long timeout, or accept that 4-bit is the practical limit.

4. **No cheaper quantum×quantum multiplication** — Cadmium source confirms modular_multiply is the best for two-quantum-register multiplication.
